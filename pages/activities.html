Outdoor Activities

The park is a haven for outdoor activities year-round. In spring, wildflowers bloom and birds are plentiful. Summer offers hiking, picnicking, boating, camping, and ranger programs. Autumn brings vibrant fall colors and opportunities for hunting. Winter is perfect for cross-country skiing, snowshoeing, ice fishing, and even ice climbing.

Popular activities include:

  • Hiking: Explore over 100 miles of trails, leading to remote trout streams, hidden bogs, and wild beaches.
  • Camping: Options range from backcountry campsites to drive-in campgrounds.
  • Water Sports: Kayaking, canoeing, and fishing are popular on both Lake Superior and the park's inland lakes and streams.

Scenic Highlights

  • Miners Castle: One of the park's most famous landmarks, accessible by a short, paved trail with stunning views of Lake Superior and Grand Island.
  • Miners Beach: A beautiful mile-long beach perfect for a relaxing day be the water or fishing at the Miners River.
  • Twelvemile Beach: This 12-mile stretch is ideal for long walks, especially at sunset, offering an unspoiled and tranquil experience.

Visitor Tips

When planning your visit, be sure to use the Munising Falls Visitor Center as your navigation point. The park is open year-round, but many roads are closed by snow in winter. Always check current conditions and plan accordingly.
